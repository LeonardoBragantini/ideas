Motorized Hotwheels

A tiny motorized Hotwheels car built with a recycled DC motor and 3D-printed parts

← Back to projects

Project Overview

Motorized Hotwheels is a small experimental project based on a toy Hotwheels car that I disassembled and modified to make it motorized.

I used a small DC motor recovered from a broken micro servo and integrated it into a custom 3D-printed base. The goal was to reuse very small components and transform a simple toy car into a working motorized prototype.

Prototype Gallery

Build Process

I disassembled the original Hotwheels toy car and recreated the lower base in 3D, including the wheel structure. The 3D-printed base was designed to fit perfectly with the original upper body of the car, keeping the external look of the toy while adding the internal mechanical parts needed for movement.

The main mechanical challenge was fitting the motor, wheels, and structure into such a small space while keeping the car compact and functional.

Main Components

  • Original Hotwheels toy car body
  • Small DC motor recovered from a broken micro servo
  • Custom 3D-printed base
  • Integrated wheel structure
  • Small-scale mechanical assembly

Main Challenge

The biggest challenge was working with extremely small components. Because of the limited space inside the car, every part had to be carefully positioned and the 3D-printed base had to be designed with very tight tolerances.

Purpose of the Prototype

This project was mainly an exercise in miniaturization, mechanical adaptation, and component reuse. Even though it is a simple prototype, it helped me experiment with small-scale design and understand how difficult it can be to fit functional electronics and mechanics into a very compact object.
