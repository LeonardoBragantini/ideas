Project Overview

Custom Hotwheels was developed as a continuation of my previous Motorized Hotwheels project, with the goal of improving the original design.

I kept the very small dimensions of a Hotwheels-style car, but I moved away from the idea of preserving the upper body of an original toy car. This gave me more freedom to redesign the structure around performance, weight reduction, and mechanical integration.

This model is still in development.

Prototype Gallery

Design Improvements

Compared to the previous version, this prototype focuses more on reducing weight and improving the transmission of power from the motor to the wheels.

The 3D-printed parts weigh only about 3 grams, making the structure extremely light. This is especially important at this scale, where even small changes in weight can noticeably affect the behavior of the car.

Mechanical Design

To improve torque transmission, I added a small gearbox recovered from the same micro steering mechanism used in the previous project. This mini gearbox allows the motor to deliver more usable power to the wheels, making the vehicle more effective despite its very small size.

Main Components

  • Custom 3D-printed lightweight structure
  • 3D-printed parts weighing about 3 grams
  • Small DC motor
  • Mini gearbox recovered from a micro steering mechanism
  • Compact wheel and drivetrain assembly
